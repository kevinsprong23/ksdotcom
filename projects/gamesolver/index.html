2048

kevinsprong.com

We need to tell the computer how to evaluate a board.

Four heuristics and their weightings were tested via grid search:

1) Win condition: losing sucks, avoid it.
2) Monotonicity: prevent tiles from getting trapped between bigger ones.
3) Smoothness: Keep tiles near ones they can merge with.
4) Open space: Leave open spaces on the board.

Open space was found to be useless - computer did best when ignoring it!

Threes!

kevinsprong.com

Evaluating a Threes board is different

Five heuristics and their weightings were tested again via grid search:

1) Win condition: losing sucks, avoid it.
2) Monotonicity: prevent tiles from getting trapped between bigger ones.
3) Smoothness: Keep tiles near ones they can merge with.
4) Open space: Leave open spaces on the board.
5) Checkerboarding: avoid getting 1's and 2's trapped by larger tiles.

In Threes open space was found to be very useful!
but checkerboarding wasn't - smoothness takes care of it.

Differences from human play

kevinsprong.com

In general, the computer will often delay making merges and let the board fill up.
This lets it dictate where tiles go, then it can merge many tiles at once

In 2048 the computer will switch the wall or corner it is attacking,
especially late when the board gets cramped

In 2048 the computer will often leave an opening such that either a 2 or a 4
in the last remaining square allows play to continue
e.g. a blank, a 2 above it, a 4 to the right, and then an 8 above and right.

In Threes! the computer can make odd moves when it knows the next 4 moves to come
where the plan depended all along on the next 4 cards all being 3's
